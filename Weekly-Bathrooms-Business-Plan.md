## 1. The verdict (read this first)

**Yes, there's a market — and there's a real gap nobody in NZ is owning properly.**

Three things make this work:

1. **The pain is real and consistent.** Every NZ renovator's own website says the same thing: a bathroom reno takes 2–6 weeks on site, the room is *completely out of action* the whole time, and timelines blow out from hidden problems, consents, and trade coordination. Homeowners hate the uncertainty more than the cost.

2. **The model is proven overseas at massive scale.** Bath Fitter (200+ locations, 40+ years), Re-Bath and Jacuzzi Bath Remodel have built a multi-billion-dollar industry in the US/Canada on exactly one promise: a productised, fixed-scope, fast bathroom (often *one day*) using acrylic/panel systems installed over or in place of the old one — "no demolition, no mess, fixed price." **Nobody runs this as a clean, single-brand productised offer in New Zealand.**

3. **Your 4-tier model is smarter than you may realise.** It lines up almost perfectly with NZ's building-consent boundary. Tiers 1–3 stay "like-for-like" and dodge consent entirely — *that* is what lets you genuinely go fast. Tier 4 (tiled) crosses into consent territory. So the tiers aren't just "good/better/best" — they're "fast because no consent" vs "premium with consent handled." That's a genuine competitive insight, not marketing.

The honest catch: **the speed story is real for Tiers 1–3. Tier 4 can't truly be "4 weeks" if it needs a fresh consent** (statutory 20 working days, realistically 6–10 weeks with RFIs). The fix is simple — sell Tier 4 as "4 weeks on site, consent sorted upfront." Covered in section 5.

## 2. The idea & positioning

**Weekly Bathrooms** is a productised bathroom renovation brand. Instead of "every job is custom, here's an open-ended quote," the customer picks a **week tier** — a fixed scope, a fixed price, and a fixed finish date. Speed, certainty, and less disruption are the product.

**Positioning line:** *Bathrooms in weeks, not months. Fixed price. Fixed date. Less pain.*

**Who it's for:** homeowners and landlords who want a tired bathroom sorted *now* without a month of chaos, an open chequebook, or a design-and-build process they don't need. Rentals, ensuites, "we're selling in spring," "the in-laws are visiting," "the shower's leaking and I just want it gone."

**Where it sits next to AVR:** AVR is the premium, custom, design-led builder. Weekly Bathrooms is the fast, packaged, accessible funnel. It captures the price- and speed-sensitive enquiries AVR currently turns away or loses on quote. Same trade engine, same Master Builder credibility — different promise. Run it as a separate brand so the "fast/affordable" message never dilutes AVR's premium one.

1. **Choice paralysis removed.** Renovators sell a process; Weekly Bathrooms sells a *menu*. Pick a week, see the price, book the date. That alone converts the "I've been meaning to do this for 2 years" homeowner.
2. **The consent-aware tiering is a moat.** You can truthfully say "Tiers 1–3 need no council consent, so we can start in days." Competitors who tile everything can't make that promise.
3. **Master Builder credibility on a fast product.** The fast/cheap end of the market is usually cowboys. Weekly Bathrooms is fast *and* backed by a Registered Master Builder with a 10-year guarantee. That combination is rare and very hard to copy.
4. **Fixed price + fixed date.** The two things the market wants most and almost nobody commits to in writing.

| **4 Weeks** | Full tiled shower, full waterproofing membrane | Premium, fully custom tiled finish | **Yes** — building consent required | from ~$24k |

### The consent reality check (important — be honest in your marketing)

NZ rule confirmed across council guidance and AVR's own blog: **a like-for-like swap or a prefab/panel shower is exempt; the moment you install a *new tiled wet-area shower with a new waterproofing membrane (Clause E3), consent is triggered.*** Statutory processing is 20 working days but RFIs commonly push bathroom consents to 6–10 weeks.

**So Tier 4 is "4 weeks on site," not "4 weeks total."** Sell it as: *we lodge and manage the consent up front; once it's granted, we're on and off site in 4 weeks.* Don't promise a tiled shower start "next week" — that's the one claim that can bite you with council and with reviews. Tiers 1–3 are where the genuine "start in days, done in weeks" speed lives. **Lead marketing with Tiers 1–3.**

## 6. Pricing strategy

- **Publish "from" prices.** Almost no NZ renovator does. Transparency is a conversion weapon for a productised brand and screens out tyre-kickers.
- **Fixed-price quote after a 20-minute site check**, locked in writing — your AVR 21-section QS process already gives you the costing rigour to do this safely.
- **Build a contingency clause** for hidden defects (rot, asbestos, dodgy old plumbing). Standard line: *"Price is fixed for the scoped work. If we open the wall and find pre-existing damage, we'll stop, show you, and quote the fix separately before continuing."* Protects the fixed-price promise without exposing you.
- **Margin:** your existing bathroom take-off runs at 50% markup — keep that. Productisation should *raise* margin over time (repeatable scopes, bulk-bought panels/trays, faster trades = less labour drift).

## 7. Operations — how you actually deliver the speed

The speed isn't magic, it's **productisation**:

1. **Standardised scopes.** Three "no-consent" packages (Tiers 1–3) with pre-set materials lists, pre-priced BOQs, and a fixed trade sequence. The QS work is done *once per tier*, not once per job.
2. **Pre-ordered stock.** Hold/standing-order the common acrylic trays, panel kits, vanities, vinyl/SPC, and tapware so there's no 2–7 day material wait per job.
3. **A tight trade pod.** A small dedicated crew (builder + plumber + electrician on call) that does *only* these packaged bathrooms, so they get fast and predictable. This is your existing contractor base (Patrick Park/DND, Popeye Zhang etc.) productised.
4. **Pre-start inspection** on every job to catch hidden issues *before* the clock starts — protects the fixed date.
5. **The AVR engine behind it.** Booking, QS, scheduling, and the Master Builder guarantee already exist. Weekly Bathrooms is a new front door onto capability you already run.

## 10. Risks & honest cautions

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Tier 4 consent timeline** undercuts the "fast" promise | Market Tiers 1–3 as the fast products; sell Tier 4 as "4 weeks on site, consent handled upfront." Never imply a tiled shower starts next week. |
| **Hidden defects** in old Kiwi homes blow the fixed date/price | Pre-start inspection + written contingency clause + small time buffer baked into each tier. |
| **Quality perception** (fast = cheap = cowboy) | Lead with Master Builder + 10-yr guarantee on everything. This is your single biggest differentiator at the fast end. |
| **Cannibalising AVR** premium jobs | Separate brand, separate funnel, fixed productised scopes. WB customers self-select *out* of bespoke design-build. |
| **Capacity** if it takes off | Dedicated trade pod that scales independently of AVR's project crews. |
| **Panel/acrylic "looks cheap"** objection | Curated finish packs (Joanna) + show real before/afters of the tile-look panels — they're genuinely good now. |
